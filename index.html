About Me

My work spans high-dimensional inference, survival and determinant modeling, and the application of machine-learning tools to complex data settings. I am currently conducting a large-scale cohort study in collaboration with Professor Hongmei Jiang and Professor Lifang Hou’s group at Northwestern’s Feinberg School of Medicine, together with external partners. I am broadly interested in methodological problems at the interface of statistics and machine learning, and how these tools can address real-world scientific and health-related questions.

Research Interests

  • High-dimensional statistics
  • Survival analysis
  • Causal inference
  • Machine learning

Selected Projects

CARDIA Proteomics & Cardiovascular Health

Currently working on statistical approaches for the CARDIA study, a large-scale cohort with over 5,000 participants and high-dimensional proteomic data. My work focuses on linking cardiovascular health profiles with proteomic signatures to improve prediction and understanding of long-term cardiovascular outcomes.

Manuscript currently in preparation as part of the CARDIA Study Writing Group.
