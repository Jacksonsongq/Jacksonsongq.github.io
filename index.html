About Me

I am a master's student in Statistics and Data Science at Northwestern University. My work spans on applied statistics, machine learning, biostatistics, and artificial intelligence. I am currently working on a large-scale collaborative project involving 35 years of cardiovascular cohort data (CARDIA), together with faculty in Northwestern’s Feinberg School of Medicine and external collaborators. I am broadly interested in methodological problems at the interface of statistics and machine learning, and how these tools can address real-world scientific and health-related questions.

Research Interests

  • High-dimensional statistics
  • Survival analysis
  • Causal inference
  • Machine learning & representation learning

Selected Projects

CARDIA Proteomics & Cardiovascular Health

I lead statistical modeling and analytical workflow development for the CARDIA proteomics project, which integrates 35 years of cardiovascular health data with over 5,000 plasma protein measurements from 5,000+ participants. My work involves cross-sectional and longitudinal analysis of cardiovascular health scores, identifying six consistent protein signals through mixed-effects modeling. I also develop survival prediction frameworks using SIS-based screening and LASSO-penalized Cox models, constructing proteomic risk scores and evaluating predictive performance through C-index, IDI, and Kaplan–Meier methods.
